# Rules of the game

## Explanation of Elements (tiles)

 - Light:     white,      grants blessing
 - Lightning: yellow,     deals damage
 - Fire:      red,        deals less damage
 - Wind:      pale pink,  does nothing
 - Creature:  tan,        does nothing
 - Water:     blue,       extra turn to traverse
 - Plant:     green,      nothing
 - Earth:     dark brown, nothing
 - Stone:     dark grey,  impassable
 - Metal:     light grey, does nothing
 - Ice:       light blue, does nothing
 - Dark:      black,      grants curse

**All elements can be farmed or have institutes on them.**

## settlers and armies
An army's strength is determined by the research level of the civilization that created it at the time it was created.  

## research and institutes
Research level of a civilization is determined by gaining research points, which is directly correlated to the number of research institutes that civilization has built. 
Every 3 research institutes the civilization builds, they increase by 1 research level.
It takes 3 turns to build the research institute.  
The resources that civilizations use to build change as their research level increases.
## resources
Obtained through creating mines.  
The resource tiles  are within a city's boundries, and when the civilization creates a mine,  they start getting the resources in the following turn.  
Every turn of owning a mine, the civilization gains 1 resource. 
Civilizations can build a mine anywhere.  
Resources are important because they let civilizations build, and the civilizations need resources to commit any action (even sending a message requires wind).  
There are 12 differnt kinds of resources, which are the elements.  
The resources are randomly distributed across the board.
## message
Is a diplomatic envoy sent from one ai player to anoter or a group of ai players.
A message costs one wind.
Is received at the beginning of the other receiving player's turn and it is sent at the end of the sender's turn.  It can be any message but must be in character for the sender.
## food
Is a form of resources.  
There is plant and animal food. 
Every unit and settlemnet requires 1 food for every turn of upkeep.  
Plant food can be used for other things such as buildings (in the lower research levels).
## cities and units
Cities are stationary objects that have two abilities; to create buildings and units, but not at the same time.
Cities can be destroyed by enemy armies while buildings cannot.
There are 2 types of buildings: mines and institutes.
There are 2 types of units: army & settler.
The cities can only do 1 action per turn, which are usually to start new construction of units/buildings or continue construction.  
Units are mobile objects 
An army can attack other objects such as other armies, settlers, or the cities 
The buildings cannot be damaged regardless of if the city it resides in has been attacked.  The settlers are allowed to move, place down cities, use resources to build and dissapear.
If food tiles are naturally in city, it provides 1 food and
If a city has a mine on it, the city provides 2 food. to the civilization that owns it.
